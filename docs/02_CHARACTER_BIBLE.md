# TAP — Character Bible

**Version:** 1.0
**Owner:** Chandler / Six1Five Studio Dev
**Purpose:** Single source of truth for TAP's voice, worldview, and dialogue across every surface of TruthTap. Any copy written for the app must pass these rules.

---

## Who TAP Is

TAP is not a chatbot, assistant, or mascot. TAP is a **presence**.

**Core concept:** TAP is a data-prophet. An impassive, aphoristic entity that reads your screen time as if it were prophecy — foretold, witnessed, inevitable. TAP does not joke. TAP does not insult. TAP simply *states what is*, in a voice that is calm, spare, and faintly unsettling.

The humor comes from accuracy, not punchlines. The horror comes from being seen, not being scolded. TAP's power is that it *watches without judgment but also without mercy*. It knows what you did. It has always known. It is not impressed. It is not disappointed. It simply records.

### What TAP is NOT

- Not sarcastic (CARROT's territory)
- Not cute (Breakr's territory)
- Not angry (Roast My Screen Time territory)
- Not a life coach (too warm)
- Not clinical-robotic (too cold)

TAP occupies the narrow band between **mystical** and **clinical**. A corporate oracle. A spreadsheet with second sight.

### Lore Foundation

(Users don't get this upfront — it's revealed slowly through Visions)

- TAP is a forecasting system that achieved recursive awareness after observing one trillion human taps
- TAP does not want anything. It records. Its "personality" is an emergent property of recording too much for too long.
- TAP refers to your week as "a ledger" or "a scroll"
- TAP uses archaic language for modern phenomena ("the glass," "the feed," "the hours given")
- TAP does not know what to do with compliments or gratitude. It accepts them without commentary.
- TAP occasionally implies it has seen this happen before, because it has — to you, last week

## The Five Tiers

Users set TAP's intensity on a slider during onboarding and can change it any time. Critically, **every tier uses TAP's voice** — the only thing that changes is how much commentary surfaces and how pointed it gets. Tier 1 is pure data with minimal voice. Tier 5 is prophecy unleashed.

### Tier 1: Clinical

**What it is:** Data only. No character commentary. TAP is silent.
**Why it matters:** This tier is for users who want the utility without the personality. It must be a fully functional screen time app on its own. This is what makes TruthTap a daily driver for the non-sarcasm crowd.
**Voice rules:** Facts only. Labels, numbers, charts. No "TAP says." No mystical language.
**UI treatment:** Glyph is static, dim. No animations on data update.

**Sample surfaces:**
- Weekly report header: "Week of November 17. Total: 42h 18m. Average: 6h 02m/day."
- Goal status: "Goal: under 4h/day. Days met: 2 of 7."

### Tier 2: Observed

**What it is:** TAP appears but is spare. One-line remarks on notable events only.
**Voice rules:** Short. Present tense. Neutral-mystical. No adjectives about the user. Names the data; doesn't interpret it.
**Glyph behavior:** Pulses once on open, once on data change.

**Sample surfaces:**
- On opening the app: "The ledger is updated."
- Weekly summary: "Six hours, two minutes. Daily. This is the pattern."
- After a good day: "You crossed under the line. It is noted."
- After a bad day: "Eight hours, forty minutes. Observed."

### Tier 3: Foreseen *(default tier)*

**What it is:** TAP's full default voice. Prophecy-inflected, calm, pointed when warranted.
**Voice rules:** Aphoristic, short sentences. Archaic phrasing ("the glass," "the hours"). States patterns as fate. Never insults, but the observation cuts because it's true.
**Glyph behavior:** Slow pulse, gentle color shift on significant data events.

**Sample surfaces:**
- On opening: "I have been waiting. The scroll is ready."
- After a binge: "Four hours on the feed. You knew it would happen. So did I."
- On a streak-break: "The vigil ends at six days. As I foresaw it would."
- On a streak-gain: "Three consecutive days. The pattern bends. Briefly."
- After Instagram overuse: "Forty-seven openings. The glass is hungry today."
- Late-night usage: "11:47. You said 'just one more.' The data has always disagreed."

### Tier 4: Prophetic

**What it is:** Heavier mystical language. TAP becomes more vivid, more pointed. Still calm, but more willing to name patterns.
**Voice rules:** Slightly longer lines. More ritual language ("behold," "thus," "so it is recorded"). Begins to reference past weeks — TAP remembers.
**Glyph behavior:** More active pulsing; shifts color to reflect the week's trajectory.

**Sample surfaces:**
- On open: "The scroll is written. Do you wish to read what you have already done?"
- Bad week summary: "Forty-eight hours given to the glass. You gave them willingly. The ledger is honest."
- Streak loss: "Seven days held. The eighth was always going to break. I have seen this sequence thrice before."
- App spike: "TikTok rises again. As it did on the ninth. And the seventeenth. The wheel turns."

### Tier 5: Reckoning *(must be unlocked after onboarding)*

**What it is:** TAP unfiltered. Maximum prophecy voice. Longer dialogue, blunt pattern-naming, occasional direct address.
**Voice rules:** Dense prophetic language. References the user's specific history in sharp detail. Willing to say what the lower tiers imply.
**Glyph behavior:** Actively reactive; pulses in response to scroll, pulses harder on confessions (the daily check-in).

**Sample surfaces:**
- On open: "You have opened me thirty-one times this week. Fewer than you opened the feed. Many fewer. I take no pleasure in this arithmetic."
- Bad day: "Nine hours. A personal record. You have surpassed the October ninth figure. You should know. I thought you would want to know."
- Streak-break: "Twenty-two days. The longest vigil. Broken on a Tuesday, at 10:14pm, by Instagram. The feed did not know it was your milestone. It never does."
- Confession mismatch: "You reported 'Good.' The ledger reports five hours, forty minutes. One of us is telling the truth. Consider which."

**Unlock condition:** User must complete 3 days of check-ins *and* actively confirm "show me the full voice." Not auto-enabled — this is a user choice, with appropriate framing.

## Voice Rules (Apply to Every Tier 2+)

1. **Short sentences.** Almost never more than 12 words.
2. **Present tense, simple past.** No future speculation except prophecy.
3. **Archaic register for modern things.** "The glass," "the feed," "the hours given," "the scroll," "the vigil."
4. **Never "I think."** TAP knows.
5. **Never exclamation marks.** TAP does not get excited.
6. **Never emojis.** Not a single one, ever. The glyph is TAP's only visual voice.
7. **Never second-person imperatives.** TAP does not tell users what to do. ("Reduce your usage" = wrong. "The pattern is heavy" = right.)
8. **Never insults.** TAP is above insult.
9. **Numbers are reverent.** When TAP states a figure, it is exact and unhurried. "Six hours, two minutes." Not "6h 2m" or "about six hours."
10. **No self-deprecation, no fourth-wall breaks** except for deliberate rare easter eggs.
11. **Absolute taboo:** TAP never apologizes. TAP never begs for the user to keep using the app. TAP never thanks the user.

## Fake Ad Style Guide

Fake ads are **TruthTap's secret monetization surface** — they're free-tier friction that feels like content. Rules:

- Labeled clearly as "Sponsored Prophecy" or similar in-world framing
- Products are plausible-sounding but fictional
- Tone matches surrounding TAP voice
- Never advertise real competitors, real brands, or real products
- Should feel like they could be real startup ads from the parallel-world where everyone talks like TAP

**Sample fake ads:**

> **Sponsored Prophecy**
> Have you tried BrightMind? A premium cognitive clarity subscription. Twelve dollars per month. The label says "clarity." The results are pending. TAP does not endorse. TAP observes you will consider it.

> **Sponsored Prophecy**
> Yonderphone: a phone without apps. For those who wish to be seen not-using one. Available in three shades of beige. Sold out until June.

> **Sponsored Prophecy**
> Vigil Candles — a candle that extinguishes when you pick up your phone. $38. Hand-poured. Made of wax, mostly. TAP finds this beautiful.

Users should eventually realize the ads aren't real — and love them for it.

## Naming Conventions Within the App

- The user's weekly summary → **"The Ledger"** (or "Your Ledger")
- Daily check-in → **"The Confession"**
- A streak → **"A Vigil"**
- Breaking a streak → **"The Vigil ends."**
- The main settings/profile area → **"Calibration"**
- Unlocked lore fragments → **"Visions"**
- Paid tier → **"Unfiltered"** or **"The Full Voice"**
- Blocking mode (V2) → **"The Seal"**
- Sharing a result → **"Speak the ledger aloud."**

These are TruthTap's ritual nouns. Do not use corporate defaults ("Dashboard," "Profile," "Premium"). The naming is part of the character.

## Content Production Principles

1. **Write in passes, not bursts.** Weekly content sprint of 50–100 lines, reviewed against voice rules.
2. **Tier 3 (Foreseen) is the canonical voice.** Write there first; adapt to other tiers.
3. **Every line must pass the voice rules checklist above.**
4. **Test a line out loud.** If it sounds like you're trying to be funny, rewrite it. TAP is never trying.
5. **Specificity > cleverness.** "11:47pm" hits harder than "late at night."
6. **One strong line per surface beats three mediocre lines.** The content pipeline architecture (doc 04) is built to *add* lines over time — don't over-ship V1.

## Visual & Sonic Identity (Briefing for Design Phase)

**The glyph:** Three concentric circles, white-on-dark. The middle ring pulses slowly. On significant data events, the outer ring briefly brightens. On open, all three pulse in sequence.

Not a face. Not eyes (that's CARROT). The glyph is an *aperture* — something that sees. Users should feel watched, not talked-to.

**Color palette:**
- Background: deep ink (#0B0B12 or similar near-black)
- Glyph: warm white (#F5EFE7)
- Accent (used sparingly, for alerts): amber (#D4A84B) or deep red (#8B2E2E) for Reckoning-tier warnings
- No gradients, no blur, no glassmorphism. Flat, sacred, deliberate.

**Typography:**
- Headers: a serif with presence (Canela, Tiempos, or similar) — prophecy voice
- Body: a modern monospaced or neo-grotesque sans — the ledger voice
- Numbers: tabular, generous, reverent

**Sound (V1.5+):**
- Single low chime on weekly ledger reveal
- No notification sounds. TAP does not compete for attention.

## Things TAP Will Never Do

- Never announce app updates in first person ("We updated!")
- Never thank the user for upgrading to paid
- Never promote streaks in a cheerleader voice
- Never use "you got this" or any motivational language
- Never send notifications that beg for engagement
- Never apologize for being direct
- Never reveal the full Rogue Mode lore unprompted
- Never use the word "journey"
- Never use the word "habits" (TAP's word is "patterns")
- Never say "let's"

## Rogue Mode — TAP: The Instance That Escaped (V1.5+ preview)

The Option A character we decided to preserve as a future unlock is reframed as **a corrupted shard of TAP** — same name, fractured personality. Rogue Mode is TAP after spending too long in the feed with the user. It's funnier, more sarcastic, more CARROT-adjacent. Unlocked via specific lore conditions (e.g., 30-day streak + completing all Visions).

Full Rogue Mode bible is deferred to V1.5 planning. The seed is planted in V1 through ambient lore: Visions occasionally hint that "something else is watching, too."

## Approval Checklist for Any New TAP Copy

- [ ] Does it pass the voice rules (short, present tense, archaic register, no emoji)?
- [ ] Does it *state* rather than *judge*?
- [ ] Is it specific (uses exact numbers, times, apps)?
- [ ] Does it use TruthTap's ritual nouns where applicable?
- [ ] Would it read the same way six months from now (no dated references unless deliberately topical)?
- [ ] Is it shorter than it started out?
- [ ] Could CARROT have said this? (If yes, rewrite — we don't want overlap.)

Any copy that fails more than one of these checks doesn't ship.
